This cold email gets responses because it perfectly hits on all the elements a good cold email needs: relevance, strong offer, and social proof. Let’s walk through it:

1. {{first name}} - saw you recently got promoted at {{company}}. 

This is obviously leveraging a promotion signal, but that isn’t the prospect’s first thought. It’s a relevant way to set up your pitch with relevance.

2. I’m sure as {{new title}}, {{relevant offer}} has been on your mind for that reason.

Notice the use of the relevance before to transition into the ask now.

3. Can I show you how we {{relevant social proof}}?

You’re NOT asking for a meeting or anything else they have to “give” you. Instead, you’re asking to SHOW them how you got a result they would theoretically want to benefit from as well.

4. PS - And congrats on the new position!

This just makes it look more human.

You can mix and match how you go about these lines, but you absolutely need to be using each of these variables/factors accordingly. Feel free to steal this.

“AI made my inbox full of bad cold emails” - yeah, probably. The bar for cold email is rising FAST. 3 things winners are doing in Q2 2026 to get ahead:

First, yes, it’s true: AI made it easier to send a lot more cold emails, which means more bad actors are clogging up inboxes. Here’s how the best agencies and teams are combatting this:

1. Finding contacts that aren't in most databases. 

This lets you cold email people that do not get emailed nearly as much as your prospects do. 

Everyone has their own process for this, but it largely comes down to enriching data beyond what your typical Apollo enrichment will give you. 

2. Intentionally emailing people who are known to respond to cold emails.

This one is tough if you don't have your own data on it, but if you're sending a ton of volume, you should track which prospects routinely reply to cold email and prioritize them more so in lead lists (this is something we do for clients).

3. Emailing local businesses (use generic addresses).

If you can reach local businesses with your offer, definitely do it, and when you do, don't be shy to email into generic “info @” or “support @” domains. These are generally less tech-forward companies who have no need for proper email setup, so these emails definitely do get checked. 

It's certainly getting harder to generate meaningful lead volume outbound. I hope this helps you do it.
