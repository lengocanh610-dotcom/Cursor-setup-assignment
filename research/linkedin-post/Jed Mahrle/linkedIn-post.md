This is the flow we use to iterate on campaigns, one metric at a time, until they’re scalable.

Step 1: Open rates

Yes, open rates are unreliable these days. And yes, open tracking hurts deliverability.

But when we first launch a campaign, we still track opens just to confirm deliverability is solid. Once it’s at 40%+, we turn off open tracking.

Step 2: Reply rates

If replies aren’t hitting at least 1-2%, we iterate on the pain point and value prop until something sticks.

Negative replies usually point you in the right direction:
- Lots of “Not the right person” → adjust persona targeting
- Lots of “We already use X” → address that objection upfront in your copy

Step 3: Positive reply rates

If you’re getting 2%+ reply rates but less than 15-25% of those are positive, it usually means your offer isn’t strong enough. 

Keep testing new offers (and how you frame them) until one resonates.

Step 4: Positive replies → Meetings

If at least half of your positive replies aren’t converting to meetings, it’s usually one of three issues:

(1) Speed to lead: Outbound replies should be treated like inbound leads: respond fast.
(2) Multi-channel follow-up: Call them. Connect on LinkedIn. Don’t just rely on email.
(3) Reply quality: Don’t overshare. Too much info gives them no reason to meet. Instead, drop a nugget and suggest a call to go deeper.

Here's my 4-step LinkedIn automation sequence:

Step 1: Blank connect (no message)

This gets way more accepts than pitching in the request.

Step 2: 1-2 sentence open-ended question 

Something low-friction and relevant to their role. Goal here is to spark a reply, not pitch. Just take your email and turn it into a text that you would send a friend. 

Step 3: Offer a helpful resource

Share something that actually helps. Could be a checklist, guide, short Loom. 

Step4: Short bump + CTA

Aquick follow-up to keep the convo alive and see if they want to chat.

This sequence is getting 30%+ reply rates across verticals.
