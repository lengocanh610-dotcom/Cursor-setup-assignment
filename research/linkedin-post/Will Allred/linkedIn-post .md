2.
How do you explain to a room of SaaS founders... they're kinda hosed? 

When I was prepping my slides on building AI defensible SaaS (for AIM's Happy Llama conference) travel seemed like the perfect place to create a relatable story. 

You imagine a trip in 2036... (hopefully Siri has a brain by then) 
AI knows your preferences
AI coordinates your schedules, flights, restaurants, cars, and your stay

You see... AI is aggregating from across the web to get the job done. (just like a person would)

Aggregation theory = the aggregator extracts the most value online.
Google = search
Amazon = commerce
Facebook = the feed

They've been winning.

AirBnB and Uber showcase two options for how this could go for tech giants.

Uber is an aggregator of rides.
Airbnb is an aggregator of unique short term rentals.

Uber (if they don't change or lean into routing) fails. They get aggregated.
They're no different than a booking (dot) com or kayak... they get aggregated by Google.

Airbnb has something different... unique data a LLM can't do the job without.

This is one of the key sources of SaaS defensibility over the next 5 years.

There will *still* be opportunities to build platforms... but, in a world where coding is a prompt away building the best platform is a race to the bottom.

You have the opportunity to build differently:

1) Go deeper on context no one else is using
2) Build reasoning layers (deep prompt networks) that create specialized labor

Combining the two creates a flywheel that helps you carve a moat.

3.
Builders have three options:

1) build the rails for execution

This is traditional SaaS as you know it. But when it runs for agents it becomes infrastructure. The future of coding makes this a rapidly commoditizing space. You can win… but it’s hard.

2) bring unique context to LLMs

Incumbents have the advantage of creating data within their databases or specialized workflows. It doesn’t mean you can’t enter. 

Ex. Anyone can access email… Lavender just decided to analyze it deeper than anyone else. Trillions of data points on what works for getting response. Should the LLM keep guessing like it’s A/B testing humans?

3) build specialized labor

If you’re using a product where the prompting is 100% yours… it better be infrastructure or it’s NGMI.

Subject matter expertise & thought patterns can be codified. These reasoning layers create products that capitalize on the same market dynamics that lead us to hire the coding firm, the expensive recruiter, consultant, even top seller. 

There should be a finger print on your product.
